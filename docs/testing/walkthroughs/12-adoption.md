# Walkthrough: The Grandmother Test

Hand someone the phone and a computer with OSmosis. Tell them nothing. Watch what happens.

---

## 12.1 — Can They Install LETHE?

1. Give a non-technical person:
   - A computer with OSmosis installed and running
   - A supported phone
   - A USB cable
2. Tell them: "This website can put a private operating system on this phone. Try it."
3. Do NOT give them any other instructions
4. Watch and take notes:
   - Where do they get stuck?
   - What do they click first?
   - Do they understand the physical button steps?
   - How long does it take?

**Pass:** They complete the install without asking for help. Under 15 minutes total.

**Fail examples:** They can't figure out where to start. They get stuck on USB debugging. Physical button instructions are confusing. They give up.

---

## 12.2 — Can They Use the Phone?

After LETHE is installed, hand them the phone and say nothing.

- [ ] Can they figure out it's the home screen?
- [ ] Can they find their apps? (Within 30 seconds)
- [ ] Can they make a phone call?
- [ ] Can they send a text?
- [ ] Can they take a photo?
- [ ] Can they browse the web?
- [ ] Can they connect to WiFi?

**Pass:** They accomplish all basic tasks without asking how. No task takes more than 2 minutes to figure out.

**Fail examples:** They don't know to swipe up. They can't find the dialer. They think the phone is broken because the home screen is "empty."

---

## 12.3 — Can They Talk to the Guardian?

1. Point at the mascot and say: "You can talk to this"
2. Watch:
   - Do they tap it or long-press?
   - Do they type or speak?
   - Does the response make sense to them?
3. Suggest they ask: "What can you do?"

**Pass:** They have a productive exchange. They learn something about the phone. They want to try more.

**Fail examples:** They don't understand the response. Agent uses jargon. Chat panel is confusing to dismiss. They think it's just a decoration.

---

## 12.4 — Do They Understand What's Different?

After 10 minutes of use, ask them:

1. "What's different about this phone?"
2. "Do you know what burner mode does?"
3. "How would you change the settings?"

**Pass:** They can articulate at least one privacy feature in their own words. They know how to get to settings.

**Fail examples:** "I don't know, it looks like a phone." They have no idea burner mode is active. They can't find settings.

---

## 12.5 — Nothing Breaks When They Explore

Let them tap around freely for 10 minutes:

- [ ] No crashes
- [ ] No dead-end screens (stuck with no back button)
- [ ] No confusing error messages
- [ ] Disabling features (Tor, burner, DMS) doesn't break the phone
- [ ] No feature requires a terminal or ADB to use
- [ ] The phone never shows jargon (IPNS, nftables, init.rc, SOCKS5, daemon)

**Pass:** They can explore freely without breaking anything or getting confused.

---

## 12.6 — Would They Use It?

The ultimate test. After 30 minutes, ask:

1. "Would you use this as your daily phone?"
2. "What would stop you?"
3. "What confused you?"

Document their answers. These are the most valuable test results.
